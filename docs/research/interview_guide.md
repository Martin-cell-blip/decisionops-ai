# Discovery Interview Guide

## Research goal

Falsify or refine two assumptions:

1. Exception investigation, rather than basic matching, is the dominant settlement pain.
2. Small merchants need a ranked decision queue with evidence, not another dashboard.

## Recruitment criteria

Recruit 7-14 participants who have taken recent action to solve the problem:

- 4-7 finance operations, bookkeeping or accounts-payable practitioners who reconciled multi-source transactions in the last 30 days.
- 3-7 e-commerce operators who changed price, inventory, assortment or campaign allocation in the last 30 days.
- Include at least two non-users who tried and abandoned an automation or BI tool.

Do not recruit people who only express general interest in AI. Prior behavior is required.

## Opening and consent

> I am researching how people currently make settlement or merchant decisions. This is not a sales call. With your permission I will take notes; I will not publish your name or company. You can skip any question or stop at any time.

## Story prompts - settlement

1. Tell me about the last reconciliation exception that took longer than expected.
2. What triggered you to investigate it? Please walk me through the files and screens in order.
3. Where did you pause, ask someone else or switch tools?
4. What evidence did you need before rejecting, recovering or paying the amount?
5. What happened when the exception crossed a month-end or involved a refund?
6. What have you already tried to automate? What broke or created more work?

## Story prompts - merchant growth

1. Tell me about the last SKU decision you changed after looking at data.
2. Which files, dashboards or people were involved? Walk me through the sequence.
3. What was the cost of waiting or making the wrong decision?
4. Which number did you distrust, and how did you verify it?
5. When did you override the dashboard or a colleague's recommendation? Why?
6. What experiment or reversible action did you use before committing?

## Four-forces probes

- **Push:** What made the old workflow unacceptable that day?
- **Pull:** What result were you trying to achieve?
- **Anxiety:** What made you hesitate to automate or change the decision?
- **Habit:** What keeps you using spreadsheets or the current tool?

## Prototype test (only after discovery)

Show one case without explaining it. Ask the participant to think aloud:

- What do you believe happened?
- What would you verify next?
- Which evidence is missing?
- Would you approve this action now? Why or why not?

Do not ask “Would you use this?” Record behavior, hesitation and missing evidence instead.

## Synthesis rule

A finding becomes a product requirement only when it is supported by:

- at least three independent participants, or
- one high-severity failure plus corroborating public/operational evidence.
